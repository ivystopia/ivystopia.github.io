# Veganism

Yes _you_ can - and should - go vegan (as much as possible)

I believe that what we call "veganism" is actually humanity's moral baseline.  
Please consider reading this document to understand my position.

## Definitions

### Carnism

- The prevailing belief system that conditions us to exploit certain animals while loving others.

### Veganism

- The moral stance that rejects carnism.
- It transcends dietary choices, and redefines humanity's entire relationship with non-human animals.
- It recognises the inherent worth of all non-human animals, advocating for their right to live free from human-imposed harm.

_According to The Vegan Society:_

> "Veganism is a philosophy and way of living which seeks to exclude — as far as is possible and practicable — all forms of exploitation of, and cruelty to, animals for food, clothing or any other purpose; and by extension, promotes the development and use of animal-free alternatives for the benefit of animals, humans, and the environment. In dietary terms, it denotes the practice of dispensing with all products derived wholly or partly from animals."

---

## Why Should I Care?

- Raising and killing non-human animals for taste or pleasure is cruel, outdated, and unethical.
- Animal farming causes suffering and is unnecessary in our modern world.
- This system is fuelled by the demand caused by individual consumers like you and me.

---

## The Ethical Imperative

- Non-human animals, like humans, can feel pain, fear, and joy.
- Billions of non-human animals suffer and die each year to fulfil the demands of human consumers.
- Humans have a unique position among animals: our intelligence makes us ethical agents - allowing us to make choices based on our morals.
- It is unethical for humans to treat non-human animals as commodities, or to exploit their lives for pleasure and profit.
- The suffering inflicted upon non-human animals due to carnism is immense.
- By renouncing carnism, you have chosen to cease participating in this cycle of exploitation, violence, and death.
- While carnism is still the legal status quo, history has often shown that the law can lag behind ethical progress.

---

## Secondary Benefits

- The health benefits of a plant-based diet, as well as the sustainability benefits of reduced animal agriculture are significant and well-documented.
- However, they are both secondary to the core goal of rethinking humanity's relationship with non-human animals.

---

## Building a Compassionate World

- By rejecting carnism, you take a stand against the systemic oppression of non-human animals and contribute to building a more compassionate world.
- You help pave the way for future generations to live in a society where empathy and respect for all life are guiding principles.

---

## Join the Movement

- **Renounce carnism.**
- Choose veganism, to reduce your demand on the system that exploits non-human animals.
- Systemic change can only begin on an individual level.
- Life is a series of choices.
- Make choices that align with veganism. You don't have to be perfect.
- It's _not your fault_ you were a carnist. It wasn't _my_ fault I was a carnist. _We didn't know._
- One by one, as individuals, we can dismantle carnism and align our actions with our ethics.
- Together, we can create a world where all beings are free from exploitation and where peace and compassion prevail.
